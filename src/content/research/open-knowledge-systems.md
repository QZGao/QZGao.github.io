Scientific and public knowledge both depend on infrastructure that is easy to overlook: citations, metadata, revision histories, and the interfaces through which contributors inspect them.

My Wikimedia work explores how small, legible tools can make that infrastructure easier to reason about. **Cite Unseen** adds source-type indicators to citations, while **CiteForge** provides a workbench for inspecting and reorganising the references on a page.

The design principle is the same one I value in scientific software: expose consequential structure without overwhelming the person who needs to act on it.
