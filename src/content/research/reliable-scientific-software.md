My earlier work as a software engineer continues to shape how I approach research. I prefer small interfaces, explicit assumptions, typed data boundaries, and outputs that remain inspectable outside the program that produced them.

That discipline is especially valuable in exploratory work. A research codebase should make it easy to distinguish a provisional experiment from a stable result, and easy to recover why a choice was made months later.

This site follows the same approach: prose is stored as structured content, interactive tools are isolated from the document layer, and each project records its status rather than quietly becoming abandonware.
